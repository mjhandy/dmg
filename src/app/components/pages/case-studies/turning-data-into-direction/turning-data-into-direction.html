Background

Nestled in the industrial corridor of southern Ontario, a small family-owned manufacturing firm had been quietly producing high-quality metal components for over 30 years. Their reputation for precision and reliability had earned them loyal B2B clients across Canada and the northeastern United States. But while their shop floor ran like clockwork, their digital presence lagged behind.

Their website—built years ago by a local freelancer—was functional but uninspired. It lacked clear calls to action, had no mobile optimization, and most critically, offered no insight into how visitors were interacting with their content. The leadership team knew they needed to modernize, but they weren’t sure where to begin. They didn’t want vanity metrics or generic dashboards—they wanted clarity. They wanted to understand their audience, improve engagement, and make informed decisions about future marketing efforts.

That’s when they reached out to Dundee Media Group.

The Challenge

From our first conversation, it was clear this wasn’t just a technical upgrade—it was a mindset shift. The firm had never used analytics before. They relied on word-of-mouth, trade shows, and cold outreach to grow their business. Their website was treated as a digital brochure, not a strategic tool.

We identified three core challenges:

  • No Analytics Infrastructure: The site had no tracking tools installed. The team had no visibility into traffic sources, bounce rates, or user behavior.
  • Unclear Engagement Goals: Without defined KPIs, it was difficult to know what “success” looked like online.
  • Limited Internal Capacity: The firm had no in-house marketing team. Any solution had to be intuitive, low-maintenance, and easy to hand off.

Our goal was to implement Google Analytics in a way that felt empowering—not overwhelming. We wanted to give them the tools to see their digital presence clearly, and the confidence to act on what they saw.

The Approach

We began with a discovery phase focused on education and empathy. Rather than diving straight into code snippets and dashboards, we asked questions:

  • What does a “good lead” look like for your business?
  • What pages do you think matter most to your visitors?
  • What would you want to know about someone who lands on your site?

These conversations helped us define meaningful engagement metrics. For this firm, success wasn’t about pageviews—it was about time spent on product pages, downloads of spec sheets, and visits to the contact form.

With these goals in mind, we mapped out a phased implementation plan:

Phase 1: Analytics Setup

  • Installed Google Analytics 4 (GA4) with enhanced measurement enabled.
  • Configured custom events to track spec sheet downloads, contact form submissions, and outbound link clicks to distributor sites.
  • Set up conversion goals aligned with their business priorities.

Phase 2: Data Hygiene & Tag Management

  • Implemented Google Tag Manager to streamline future tracking updates.
  • Created a clean data layer to ensure consistent event firing across devices.
  • Filtered out internal traffic and bot activity to preserve data integrity.

Phase 3: Monthly Reporting Framework

  • Designed a custom Looker Studio dashboard tailored to their KPIs.
  • Created automated monthly reports with visual summaries of engagement trends.
  • Trained their operations lead on how to interpret the data and spot anomalies.

Throughout the process, we kept the language simple and the visuals clean. No jargon. No clutter. Just actionable insights.

The Solution

The final analytics setup was more than just a technical integration—it was a strategic foundation.

Before working with Dundee, we were flying blind online. We didn’t know who was visiting our site, what they cared about, or how to improve.

Key Features:

  • Custom Event Tracking: Every spec sheet download, contact form submission, and distributor link click was tracked as a conversion event.
  • Audience Segmentation: We built segments for Canadian vs. U.S. visitors, mobile vs. desktop users, and new vs. returning traffic.
  • Behavior Flow Analysis: The firm could now see how users moved through the site—from landing pages to product details to contact forms.
  • Monthly Engagement Reports: Delivered as PDFs and interactive dashboards, these reports highlighted trends, flagged drop-offs, and suggested optimizations.

We also added annotations to their dashboard to mark key events—like trade show dates or product launches—so they could correlate spikes in traffic with real-world activity.

The Results

Within three months of launch, the firm saw measurable improvements in both engagement and confidence:

  • Contact Form Submissions Increased by 42%: Thanks to clearer CTAs and better visibility into user behavior.
  • Spec Sheet Downloads Rose by 58%: Indicating stronger interest in product details and technical specs.
  • Bounce Rate Dropped by 35%: Especially on mobile, where navigation had previously been clunky.
  • Team Insights: The monthly reports helped the sales team prioritize follow-ups based on geographic traffic patterns and product interest.

But perhaps the most meaningful result was intangible: the leadership team began to see their website as a living part of their business—not just a static brochure. They started asking smarter questions, testing new ideas, and using data to guide decisions.
